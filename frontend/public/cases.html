AI In Cinema

9 cases where Artificial Intelligence shapes the screen

From de-aged legends to synthetic actors — each film reveals how technology reshapes truth, emotion, and cinematic identity.

// Multiverse Aesthetics

Everything Everywhere All At Once (2022)

The Daniels used AI-driven editing tools to blend multiple universes and faces. The film visually captures the fragmented logic of digital identity — chaotic yet deeply human.

// AI Performance Capture

Avatar: The Way of Water (2022)

Neural networks were trained to predict actors’ facial muscles underwater, merging human performance and AI to create a seamless emotional realism. “AI and deep learning are becoming part of the filmmaking craft. We don’t use them to replace actors — we use them to reveal more truth in their performances.” - James Cameron

// AI Ethics & Visual Realism

The Creator (2023)

Gareth Edwards combined real-world footage with AI compositing tools to generate futuristic landscapes. The film explores coexistence between artificial and organic life — on screen and behind it.

// AI Face Reconstruction

Indiana Jones and the Dial of Destiny (2023)

AI de-aged Harrison Ford by analyzing decades of archival footage. This fusion of memory and data created a version of the actor who never truly existed — only remembered by algorithms.

// Generative AI for visual effects

The Eternaut (2024)

Used generative AI for visual effects, including the creation of a complex building-collapse sequence, helping reduce production time and costs.

// Digital de-aging

Pedro Páramo (2024)

Used Vanity AI for digital de-aging, allowing characters to appear younger without extensive makeup or traditional VFX workflows.

// De-aging and Age transformation

Here (2024)

Used Metaphysic AI for de-aging and age transformation, enabling actors to portray their characters across multiple decades while maintaining the same cast.

// Fully AI

Mork (2026)

Created entirely with Higgsfield Cinema Studio, enabling a single filmmaker to produce a 22-minute action-horror film in one week without a traditional production crew, significantly reducing production time, costs, and post-production requirements.

// Fully AI

Dreams of Violets (2026)

Created as a fully AI-generated feature film, using AI-generated visuals, characters, and voice transformations with a production budget of under $2,000.
